CapCut Terms, Icons, and Recommended Export Settings

Use this quick reference guide to understand common CapCut terms, editing icons, and recommended export settings for social media videos. This topic is intended to help beginner users quickly find information while editing.

Common Capcut Editing Terms

The following terms are commonly used while editing videos in CapCut. Understanding these terms can make the editing process easier and help users follow instructions more confidently.

Table 1: Editing Terms
Term Definition Why It Matters
Timeline The main editing area where clips are organized Used for arranging and editing videos
Trim Shortening a clip by removing unwanted footage Helps remove unnecessary sections
Split Dividing a clip into separate parts Makes editing specific sections easier
Transition Effect between clips Helps videos flow more smoothly
Text Overlay Words displayed on a video Used for captions or titles
Audio Track Music or sounds added to a video Improves engagement and pacing
Effect Visual enhancement added to clips Improves video appearance
Export Saving the finished video Required before posting online
Frame Rate Number of frames displayed per second Affects video smoothness
Resolution Video quality level Impacts video clarity

Common CapCut Icons

CapCut uses icons to represent editing tools. The following icons are commonly used when creating and editing videos.

Table 2: Icons
Icon Tool Name Purpose
+ Add Clip Imports videos or photos
T Text Adds captions or titles
🎵 Audio Adds music and sounds
✂ Split Cuts clips into sections
⇄ Transition Adds effects between clips
⭐ Effects Applies visual enhancements
↑ Export Saves completed video

Recommended Export Settings for Social Media

Choosing the correct export settings can improve video quality and ensure videos display properly on social media platforms.

Table 3: Export Settings
Platform Recommended Resolution Aspect Ratio Suggested Frame Rate
TikTok 1080p 9:16 30 fps
Instagram Reels 1080p 9:16 30 fps
Instagram Feed 1080p 4:5 30 fps
YouTube Shorts 1080p 9:16 30–60 fps

Common Editing Problems and Quick Fixes

The following problems are commonly experienced by beginner users while editing in CapCut.

Table 4: Troubleshooting
Problem Possible Cause Quick Fix
Cannot access photos Permissions disabled Allow photo access in settings
App freezes Too many apps open Restart CapCut
Export fails Low storage Free storage space
Audio does not match clips Incorrect placement Adjust audio position in timeline
