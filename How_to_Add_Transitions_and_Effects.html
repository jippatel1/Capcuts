How to Add Transitions and Effects

Learn how to add transitions and effects in CapCut to create smoother scene changes and improve the visual appearance of your video.

Before starting this task, complete How to Import, Arrange, and Trim Video Clips. Make sure your project contains at least two clips in the timeline.
Transitions and effects can help make videos more visually appealing and engaging. Transitions create smoother movement between clips, while effects add visual enhancements that can improve the overall style of a video.
  1. Open your CapCut project.
    The project opens in the editing workspace.
  2. Locate the small transition icon between two clips in the timeline.
    A transition can only be added between clips, so your project must contain at least two clips.
    The transition icon appears between the selected clips.
  3. Tap the transition icon.
    The transitions menu opens.
  4. Browse the available transition categories.
    Common categories include basic, camera, slide, and motion transitions.
    Available transition options are displayed.
  5. Select a transition and preview it.
    Previewing allows you to see how the transition will appear before applying it.
    The transition preview plays.
  6. Tap the checkmark to apply the transition.
    The transition is added between the clips.
  7. Adjust the transition duration if necessary.
    Shorter durations often work better for fast-paced social media videos.
    The transition timing is updated.
  8. Tap Effects from the toolbar.
    The effects menu opens.
  9. Select either Video Effects or Body Effects.
    Video Effects modify the appearance of clips, while Body Effects add motion-based enhancements.
    Effect categories appear.
  10. Browse and preview available effects.
    Choose effects that support the purpose of your video rather than distracting from it.
    The selected effect preview plays.
  11. Tap the checkmark to apply the effect.
    The effect is added to the video.
  12. Preview the video to review the transitions and effects.
    Watch the entire sequence to make sure the edits appear smooth and consistent.
    The edited video plays in the preview window.
You have successfully added transitions and effects to your video. Your project now includes smoother scene changes and visual enhancements that can improve viewer engagement.
